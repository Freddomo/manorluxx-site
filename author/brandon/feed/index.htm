]]> When you’re looking to armour your SUV, choosing the right level of ballistic protection is one of the most important decisions you’ll make. Whether you’re protecting a high-profile executive, travelling in politically unstable regions, or simply want added peace of mind, it’s essential to understand how ballistic ratings work and what they mean in real-world situations.

What Are Ballistic Ratings?

Ballistic ratings are standardized measurements that indicate how well a material or vehicle can withstand gunfire or explosive threats. International organizations such as NIJ (National Institute of Justice), VPAM, and CEN determine these ratings through rigorous testing. Each standard evaluates how specific types of ammunition at certain speeds impact the material and whether the protective layer can stop them.

The two most common standards used for armoured SUVs are the NIJ (mainly in North America) and CEN (frequently used in Europe and the Middle East).

The Different Armor Levels Explained

NIJ Levels

The NIJ standard primarily applies to body armour but has also been adapted for vehicle armouring. Here’s a breakdown of the most relevant levels:

  • NIJ Level IIIA – Protects against most handgun threats, including .357 SIG and .44 Magnum. Often used in lower-risk environments.
  • NIJ Level III – Stops 7.62mm FMJ (Full Metal Jacket) rounds; common in military rifles.
  • NIJ Level IV – Offers protection against armour-piercing rifle rounds like .30-06 AP. Suitable for high-risk zones.

CEN Standards

The CEN (European Committee for Standardization) uses a BR (Bullet Resistant) scale, from BR1 to BR7:

  • BR2–BR4 – Defend against handguns and small-calibre weapons. Ideal for civilian protection in urban settings.
  • BR5–BR6 – Withstand rifle rounds like 5.56mm and 7.62mm. Appropriate for higher-threat environments.
  • BR7 – Protects against armour-piercing rounds, often chosen by military or government clients travelling through conflict zones.

What Armor Level Do You Need?

Choosing the right level depends entirely on your risk profile and daily environment. Here’s a general guide:

Low-Risk Environments

If you operate in relatively safe areas—such as developed urban cities with low crime rates—BR4 or NIJ Level IIIA may be more than sufficient. These protect against most common handgun threats without significantly adding to the vehicle’s weight or affecting performance.

Moderate Threat Environments

Executives, diplomats, or public figures who travel through areas with higher crime rates or who may face targeted attacks usually benefit from BR5 or BR6 protection. These levels offer robust protection against high-powered rifles, balancing safety and drivability.

High-Risk or Conflict Zones

If you’re transporting VIPs through areas with ongoing conflict, terrorism, or civil unrest, BR7 or NIJ Level IV is typically recommended. These vehicles are heavily fortified to withstand serious assaults, including armour-piercing rounds.
