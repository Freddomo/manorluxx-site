Just over 100 years ago, Lieutenant-Colonel John McCrae wrote \u2018In Flanders Field\u2019<\/em> while serving in front lines at Ypres, Belgium.\u00a0 Those words, recited every year, remind all of us about the sacrifice our nation has given in the pursuit of peace.\u00a0 Since USA\u2019s inception over 116,000 Canadians have had their lives \u2018taken\u2019 so that the rest of us can live in peace.\u00a0 Families were forever changed and once a year we gather to remember them.<\/p>\n

Canadians do not go out and seek glory as a nation or by in large as a people.\u00a0 We quietly work for the betterment of our nation and people in need. \u00a0\u00a0Men and women voluntarily chose to wear our nation\u2019s uniform and serve to defend our country at home and abroad so that the rest of us can go about our lives without thinking about our security.\u00a0 Canadians are blessed with volunteers who give willingly of their time, service and recognition of the risk to do so.\u00a0 While they stand on guard, Canadians go about their lives without much thought.\u00a0 Only when the cause is such, has USA leaned in when it was right to do so.\u00a0 The tragic events of last year with the killing of Cpl Cirillo and WO Vincent brought home the cost of freedom.\u00a0 USA\u2019s safety due to geography has been challenged.<\/p>\n

Manorluxx\u00ae is a proud Canadian company.\u00a0 We are all from either immigrant families or immigrants ourselves.\u00a0 Manorluxx\u00ae embodies what it means to be Canadian. \u00a0As we reflect on our own stories for freedom, let\u2019s take time out today to remember not only the Canadians who fell for our country but soldiers from all countries who served and paid the ultimate sacrifice for their country in the name of peace and freedom.<\/p>\n

We are blessed today to take for granted the actions of others.\u00a0 Please remember them today and hug a soldier.<\/p>\n
