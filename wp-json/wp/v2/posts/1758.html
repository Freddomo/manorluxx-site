An excerpt from the News Article: <\/span><\/strong><\/p>\n

Bullet casings the size of your pinky finger litter the floor of a small room inside an anonymous brown-brick industrial building in Toronto\u2019s suburbs. A sign in the hall outside reads: \u201cLive Ammunition Test. Do Not Enter.\u201d<\/p>\n

A collection of squares of dark-grey steel, about the size of coffee-table books, are scattered on a workbench. Some are punctuated with small, white dents and pockmarks. Others have bullet-sized holes. A pane of inch-thick glass, spider-webbed but not shattered, leans against the wall<\/p>\n

This is the stuff of which bulletproof cars are made. On the factory floor down the corridor, the staff of Inkas Armoured Vehicle Manufacturing\u2013one of several low-profile companies in the Toronto area in the business\u2013are stripping apart and retrofitting brand-name SUVs and luxury sedans, mostly bound for overseas customers.<\/p>\n
