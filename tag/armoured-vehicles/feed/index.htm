Each state has its own laws governing the ownership and operation of armored vehicles. These regulations can encompass everything from the process of registering the vehicle to the specifics of window tinting. Typically, owners must comply with federal safety standards that apply to all motor vehicles, along with additional, localized criteria—such as proof of purpose for the armored vehicle.

Before acquiring an armored SUV, consult your state’s Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) or an experienced legal advisor to clarify any special permits, labels, or documentation you might need. Differences from State to State

With so many different rules, the variations between states can be significant. For instance, some states may require specialized licensing for drivers who operate heavily armored vehicles, while others may have stringent requirements for ballistic-resistant glass. In regions with higher traffic congestion, additional guidelines might exist regarding maximum vehicle dimensions or weight limits.

Because regulations differ substantially across the country, vehicle owners sometimes find themselves adapting to new requirements if they travel frequently between states. If you plan to move or use your armored vehicle across various regions, it pays to prepare by reviewing the relevant laws or contacting local authorities.

To maintain the highest safety standards, manufacturers like Troy Armoring rigorously test their vehicles to ensure they meet or exceed recognized industry benchmarks. Choosing the Right Armored SUV

An armored SUV is popular for those seeking comfort and robust security on the road. Adding interior space can accommodate family members, staff, or equipment while offering superior protection. However, selecting the right model involves more than just comparing prices. Consider the vehicle’s armor level, which relates to its protective capabilities, along with any specific local regulations on modifications—like ballistic glass thickness or run-flat tires—that might affect its eligibility for registration in your state.

This includes monitoring oil levels, checking for leaks, and ensuring that the cooling system is functioning properly to prevent overheating. Regular oil changes and filter replacements are also essential to maintain engine health.

Explore the range of our durable and high-performance armored SUVs here.

2. Tire Maintenance is Critical

Armored vehicles have specially designed tires to handle extra weight and, in some cases, continue running even when punctured. Regularly inspect tires for wear and tear, maintain correct tire pressure, and have a schedule for tire rotation. This will not only extend the life of the tires but also ensure your SUV handles as expected in critical situations.

3. Suspension and Brake Systems

The suspension and brake systems of an armored SUV undergo more stress due to the vehicle’s increased weight. Regular inspections are necessary to ensure that shock absorbers, struts, and brake pads are in optimal condition. Replacing worn parts promptly is essential to maintain the performance and safety of the vehicle.

4. Checking Electrical Systems

The electrical system in an armored SUV powers critical security features. Regular checks of the battery, alternator, and wiring help prevent failures. Pay special attention to any unusual electrical behavior, as this can be a sign of deeper issues.

5. Armor Integrity Inspections

The armor of your SUV is what provides the high level of protection. Regular inspections for any damage or wear are essential. Look for signs of rust or corrosion, particularly in hidden areas, and ensure that all armored panels and glass are secure and intact.

Ballistic Armor

First on the list is the ballistic armor. This isn’t your standard metal plating. Armored vehicles are engineered with composite materials such as high-hardened steel, Kevlar, and even composite ceramics. This ensures the car is a mobile fortress, safeguarding its occupants from ballistic attacks.

Run-flat Tires

Imagine a high-pressure situation where a tire blowout could mean life or death. Armored cars are equipped with run-flat tires that can keep the vehicle moving for a considerable distance, even after being punctured. This is crucial for evading attackers and getting to safety.

Reinforced Suspension

The added weight from the armor needs a robust support system. Reinforced suspensions ensure the vehicle remains stable, maneuverable, and responsive even with the additional load. This is critical during high-speed evasive maneuvers.

Secure Passenger Compartment

For added security, armored vehicles often feature a secure passenger compartment that acts as a safe room. This fortified space is constructed with reinforced materials and can be sealed in emergencies.
